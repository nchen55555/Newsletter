An Organic Introduction

A few of you might remember when we profiled Pathlight Ventures and took a closer look at one of their recent investments: Volca AI. What began as a quick scan of Pathlight’s LinkedIn announcement turned into a deep dive into Volca’s thesis, their Forbes feature, and even a conversation with co-founder Brendan Kazanjian. That journey now brings me to writing the official company profile on Volca for The Niche!

What excites me most is how organically my introduction to Volca unfolded. While researching Pathlight’s portfolio, I kept coming back to Volca’s Forbes article. It struck a chord personally: my parents, as landlords in the real estate market, are constantly voicing frustrations about how difficult it is to find reliable, validated home service providers. Whether it’s fixing a dishwasher in a rental unit or repairing a roof after a storm, it’s just been difficult for them to find the right people, validated for the job. Just last week, a co-worker posted on Slack asking everyone in “nyc-ask-anything” if there were any referrals to home service businesses that specialized in window repair.

The Home Services Market Today

These everyday struggles from landlords, homeowners, or even colleagues highlights the very gap Volca is aiming to close. Volca’s bet is to revolutionize the way people connect to small businesses and home services by automating and embedding referrals in the fundamental GTM strategy for these businesses. Indeed, for most small businesses operating in the home services space, marketing and advertising is a patchwork of hit-or-miss tactics. For most, it’s Google ads and SEO, Yelp, Angi, or Nextdoor to generate leads; experimenting with direct marketing like emails or SMS (though the latter is limited by consent laws); or sticking up yard signs, truck decals, or local billboards.
